Conglong Xu

Researcher in Machine Learning • Optimization • Quantitative Methods

I am Conglong Xu, a doctoral candidate in Mathematics at George Washington University, with interdisciplinary graduate training in applied economics and law. My research focuses on leveraging geometric and topological methods to develop scalable algorithms for nonconvex optimization and applying them in large-scale learning systems. In particular, I work on Riemannian optimization, principled algorithm design, and the development of reproducible, research-grade codebases for modern machine learning applications.

Selected Research Projects

  • Riemannian Stochastic Gradient Descent (rie-sgd-code)
    Implementation of Riemannian SGD and a solver to weighted low-rank approximation based on Riemannian SGD. Deployment of the solver within a recommender system. Performance validation against real-world Netflix ratings data for movie recommendation tasks.
  • Manifold-based Linear Classification (codebase-linear-classification)
    Research-grade implementation of Riemannian-optimization-based linear classifiers with modular experiment pipelines and reproducible benchmarking.
